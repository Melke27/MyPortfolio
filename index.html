# About Me - Melkamu Wako

Introduction

I'm Melkamu Wako, a dedicated and forward-thinking Computer Science and Engineering student at Adama Science and Technology University (ASTU). My passion for technology extends across multiple domains including web development, competitive programming, robotics, and cybersecurity. I view every line of code not just as syntax, but as a step toward solving real-world problems, building a better digital experience, and growing into a responsible tech leader. This portfolio outlines my journey, skills, achievements, and aspirations.

Academic Background

Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering at ASTU, I have consistently maintained a strong academic performance. My coursework has given me a solid foundation in computer systems, algorithms, database systems, networking, and software engineering. I complement this formal education with continuous self-learning through online platforms and hands-on projects.

Competitive Programming

One of my core strengths lies in competitive programming. I actively participate in coding contests on platforms such as Codeforces and LeetCode. I specialize in solving algorithmic problems that demand high proficiency in logic, data structures, and algorithmic thinking. My expertise in C++ has been pivotal in tackling time-sensitive and memory-constrained challenges efficiently.

Platforms I Use:

  • Codeforces: Regular contestant, solving problems involving dynamic programming, greedy algorithms, graphs, and more.
  • LeetCode: Practicing advanced problems related to interviews and real-world use cases.

These experiences sharpen my problem-solving skills and equip me to think analytically and optimize solutions under pressure.

Web Development Skills

As a full-stack web developer, I'm deeply passionate about building modern, responsive, and user-friendly web applications. My projects reflect attention to detail, usability, and performance.

Robotics & Team Alpha Experience

As an active member of Team Alpha Robotics ASTU, I had the honor of contributing to our 2nd place victory at the 2025 African Robotics Championship. My role involved developing automation software, integrating sensor inputs, and writing scratch code to control the robot's behaviors.

Key Contributions:

  • Developed a scratch-based control system for robot movement and logic execution.
  • Collaborated with mechanical and electronics teams to ensure smooth integration.
  • Participated in testing, debugging, and refining the robot's software for live performance.

This experience sharpened my teamwork, interdisciplinary communication, and practical problem-solving abilities.
